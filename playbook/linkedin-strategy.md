# LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

## Overview

This playbook outlines a practical approach to building and scaling LinkedIn content for B2B SaaS, based on analysis of leading operators and creators.

---

## Core Strategy

### 1. Create Opinionated Content

Top-performing content takes a clear stance instead of staying neutral.

Example:

* "Take a stance. Defend it with data." (source: [Ross Simmonds](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_the-biggest-lie-in-b2b-marketing-is-that-activity-7444351668688699393-K8cZ), 2026)

---

### 2. Focus on Zero-Click Value

Deliver value directly in the post without requiring users to click.

Example:

* "Earn attention by being worth paying attention to." (source: [Amanda Natividad](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_a-friend-asked-me-whats-really-driving-my-activity-7443322215036547072-wXKl), 2026)

---

### 3. Use Data to Build Authority

Posts with original data and benchmarks perform better.

Example:

* (source: [Kyle Poyar](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-poyar_who-owns-growth-heres-the-latest-data-activity-7444735295796838400-QiS6), 2026)

---

### 4. Simplicity and Structure Win

High-performing posts are easy to read and structured.

Example:

* (source: [Justin Welsh](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_a-lot-of-business-comes-down-to-who-you-know-activity-7436029984021991424-N8j4), 2026)

## Where Experts Disagree

### 1. Opinion vs Data

* Ross Simmonds -> strong opinions drive engagement
* Kyle Poyar -> data builds authority

**My take:**
Best content combines both. Opinion attracts attention, data builds trust.

---

### 2. Personal Story vs Tactical Content

* Justin Welsh -> storytelling and personal insights
* Elena Verna -> tactical and execution-focused content

**My take:**
Use storytelling for reach and tactical content for credibility.

---

### 3. Brand vs Performance Focus

* Dave Gerhardt -> brand and awareness
* Peep Laja -> conversion and messaging precision

**My take:**
Brand attracts, conversion captures demand. Both are required.

---

## What I Rejected and Why

### 1. Highly introspective content (Chris Walker)

Rejected because it lacks direct applicability for LinkedIn growth strategy and is not consistently actionable.

---

### 2. Overly generic networking advice

Rejected because it does not differentiate and provides low strategic value.

---

## My Original Ideas

### Hybrid Content System

Rotate between:

* Opinion-based content
* Data-driven content
* Storytelling content

**Why it works:**
This balances engagement, authority, and relatability while preventing content fatigue.

---

## Weaknesses of This Playbook

* Based only on public content, not internal performance data
* May not apply equally across all industries
* Assumes consistency in posting and content creation
