# Amanda Natividad - LinkedIn Posts Analysis

## Post 1

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_a-friend-asked-me-whats-really-driving-my-activity-7443322215036547072-wXKl  
Date: March 27, 2026

Content:  
A friend asked me what's really driving my obsession with Zero Click Marketing.

Is it getting more likes? Growing an audience?

Not really. It's because of a chip on my shoulder.

I didn't come up through traditional marketing training. I studied communications, worked in journalism, wanted to do food writing, and kind of stumbled into marketing. So I didn't enter this field thinking like a marketer first. I got here from having learned how to write and report, how to source things properly, and how to consume media with a critical eye.

Now Zero Click Marketing doesn't feel like a new idea to me. It's about making things people actually want to consume. Things that are useful or interesting on their own. That don't need a click to justify their existence.

For a long time, I had to defend that way of thinking.

I don't really care whether something can ship. I care whether it deserves to.

I really think Zero Click Marketing matters more than ever today.

Earn attention by being worth paying attention to.

Key Insight:  
High-performing content should deliver value without requiring users to leave the platform, focusing on earning attention rather than forcing clicks.

Why it matters:  
For B2B SaaS, zero-click content increases reach, engagement, and trust on LinkedIn, reducing reliance on external traffic and improving organic distribution.

Pattern observed:  
Uses personal storytelling to introduce a broader marketing concept, ending with a strong and memorable principle.

## Post 2

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_most-people-think-linkedin-has-one-algorithm-activity-7439328384398204928-dNRC  
Date: March 2026

Content:  
Most people think LinkedIn has one algorithm.

But according to a new analysis, the platform may actually be running two systems:  
1) One decides whether your post should be considered for the feed  
2) The other decides how it ranks

The post compares social media to a party, where you should join conversations before starting your own.

Key Insight:  
LinkedIn content performance depends on both initial relevance (getting into the feed) and engagement (ranking within it), requiring a more nuanced content strategy.

Why it matters:  
For B2B SaaS, this means content must both signal relevance early and generate engagement to scale distribution, reinforcing the importance of conversation-driven content.

Pattern observed:  
Uses a simple breakdown (two systems) combined with a relatable analogy (social media as a party) to make a complex concept easy to understand.
