# Dave Gerhardt - LinkedIn Posts Analysis

## Post 1

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_big-news-out-of-exit-five-hq-for-the-first-activity-7444700076901298176-xIgU  
Date: March 2026

Content:  
Post describing an experiment with out-of-home advertising in a non-target market (golf event sponsorship). Highlights that even if the audience is not directly relevant, the real ROI comes from sharing the initiative on LinkedIn and generating impressions there.

Key Insight:  
Distribution through LinkedIn can amplify offline marketing efforts, sometimes generating more value than the original campaign itself.

Why it matters:  
For B2B SaaS, LinkedIn can act as a primary distribution layer, turning even small or experimental campaigns into high-reach content opportunities.

Pattern observed:  
Uses storytelling and a real-life experiment to demonstrate a broader marketing principle about distribution and attention.

## Post 2

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_linkedin-is-the-number-one-channel-for-b2b-activity-7439654089778462720--k4c  
Date: March 2026

Content:  
Post summarizing a podcast with LinkedIn's VP of Marketing, covering topics such as LinkedIn ads performance, brand vs performance marketing, attribution challenges, and the reality that most buyers are not in-market.

Key Insight:  
Effective B2B marketing requires balancing brand and performance, while understanding that most of the audience is not ready to buy at any given time.

Why it matters:  
For B2B SaaS, this reinforces the importance of long-term brand building and consistent content, rather than relying only on short-term conversion tactics.

Pattern observed:  
Combines authority (expert guest), data points, and structured bullet points to deliver high-value insights in a concise format.
